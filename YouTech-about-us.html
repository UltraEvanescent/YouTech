ABOUT US

At YouTech, we embrace the absurdity of planned obsolescence and dive headfirst into a world where consumerism and self-sabotage collide. As a fictitious tech company, we aim to draw attention to the extreme lengths some companies go to ensure perpetual demand for their products. With an exaggerated parody of Apple in our sights, we present a satirical commentary on the direction of global consumerism.

Our premise is simple yet audacious. We offer a range of TVs and phones that are intentionally in disrepair, transforming these dysfunctional devices into fashionable and desirable items. By turning planned obsolescence into an art form, we challenge conventional notions of product quality and longevity.

But it doesn't stop there. At YouTech, we take the concept of self-sabotage to the extreme, turning it into a fashionable and interactive experience. With our unique service, customers can browse through our catalog of working devices, selecting the one they desire. But instead of simply purchasing it, we provide a range of branded tools of destruction – hammers, baseball bats, and more – for customers to use and participate in the act of sabotage themselves.

Why, you may ask? Because we believe that obliterating perfectly functional devices in the name of fashion is the ultimate statement against the manipulative tactics of corporations. It is a playful rebellion, a tongue-in-cheek way to shine a spotlight on the absurdity of our consumer-driven society.

Through our exaggerated parody, we hope to ignite a conversation about the disposable nature of our possessions and the perpetual cycle of consumption that permeates modern society. YouTech is not just a company; it is a social commentary wrapped in satire and wit.

Join us in questioning the status quo, challenging the norms, and pushing the boundaries of what is considered acceptable in the realm of consumerism. Together, let's explore the outrageous and expose the flaws of a system that thrives on our insatiable desire for the new.
